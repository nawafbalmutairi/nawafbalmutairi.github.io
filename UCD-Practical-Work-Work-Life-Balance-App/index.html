UCD Practical Work
Work-Life Balance App

User-Centred Design case study: personas, MoSCoW requirements, interface sketches, wireflow diagrams, interactive prototype, and cognitive walkthrough evaluation for a student productivity app.

Nawaf Almutairi
BSc Computer Science
Northumbria University
Tools: Figma · Canva
App Concept
A simple, easy-to-use app that helps working students manage their tasks, deadlines, and schedules in one place. It provides personalized reminders, progress tracking, and a calendar view to stay organized and reduce stress.
Target Users: Working students aged 18-25 who need a simple tool to manage busy schedules

— 1. Personas & Scenarios
Persona 1
Sarah Jane
22-year-old business student working part-time at a retail store. Lives in a shared apartment, relies on smartphone and basic laptop. Struggles with overlapping schedules and late-night fatigue.
Goals
Graduate with good grades
Maintain financial independence
Improve time management skills
Frustrations
Scheduling conflicts between work and classes
Late-night fatigue affecting study quality
Difficulty finding focused study time
"Finally, I don't have to stress about what to study or when to study. This app makes it easy to plan my day without feeling overwhelmed!"
Persona 2
James Carter
22-year-old undergraduate working part-time as a barista. Uses mid-range laptop and smartphone. Main problem is juggling overlapping deadlines and unpredictable work hours.
Goals
Complete degree while building portfolio
Manage overlapping deadlines efficiently
Reduce stress during high workload
Frustrations
Difficulty setting boundaries between work and study
Overlapping deadlines causing stress
Lack of structured plan for commitments
"This app helps me stay on top of things. Now I can see all my deadlines in one place and prioritize my work without the usual panic."
Persona 3
Alex Morgan
24-year-old final-year law student working part-time as a legal intern. Lives with parents, commutes daily. Prefers reliable, straightforward tools. Cautious about adopting new technology.
Goals
Gain practical experience while maintaining grades
Develop time-blocking strategies for study
Minimize last-minute cramming before exams
Frustrations
Limited study time due to long internship hours
High stress during exams and deadlines
Difficulty balancing academic and work life
"With this app, I can finally block time for study and focus without distractions. No more last-minute cramming!"

— 5. Evaluation Plan
Cognitive Walkthrough
Evaluators simulate a new user's experience step-by-step, asking: Will the user know what to do? Will they see how to do it?
Observation
Evaluators observe users during the walkthrough, noting where they encounter problems or hesitation.
Think-Aloud Protocol
Users explain their thoughts while completing tasks, helping evaluators understand their reasoning and difficulties.
Post-Test Questionnaire
Users provide feedback on their experience after the walkthrough, offering subjective insights into usability.

Passed Well
Login (T1), Add Task (T2), Set Reminder (T4), Calendar View (T5), and Logout (T9) all passed all four cognitive walkthrough questions — users found these flows clear, visible, and with appropriate feedback.
Critical Issue: Edit Task (T6)
Users could not find the Edit option beside each task, the edit form was unclear, and there was no visible save/edit button. This is the most significant usability issue discovered — 3 out of 4 questions failed.
Feedback Gaps
Tasks T3 (priority setting), T7 (progress tracking), and T8 (profile editing) lacked clear feedback after user actions. Users completed the tasks but were unsure if their changes were saved or applied.
